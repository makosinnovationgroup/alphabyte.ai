# Voice & Tone

How Alphabyte sounds. Sourced from Brand Guide §1.0.

## The one-line summary

**Calm, composed, direct. The steady hand in the chaos.** Technical expertise delivered in plain language, without hype.

If you're writing copy and you're not sure whether it sounds like Alphabyte, read it aloud. Does it sound like a thoughtful senior engineer explaining something? Good. Does it sound like a LinkedIn post or a pitch deck? Rewrite.

## Brand values — what they mean for writing

The brand guide lists four values. Here's what each one actually means when you're at the keyboard:

### Proven Expertise ("Tried & Tested")
Write from a position of having done the thing before. State capabilities plainly, without peacocking.

- ✅ "We've deployed this pattern across finance, healthcare, and logistics clients."
- ❌ "Our world-class team brings unparalleled expertise to every engagement."

### Innovative ("For the Greater Good")
Change is framed as useful, not revolutionary. Avoid the word "revolutionary."

- ✅ "We build AI systems that make real work easier."
- ❌ "We're revolutionizing how enterprises leverage transformative AI."

### Empowering ("Human Connection")
Write *to* the reader, not at them. Second person ("you") works. Assume the reader is smart.

- ✅ "You already have the data. We help you put it to work."
- ❌ "We empower our valued clients to unlock their data potential."

### Intentional ("Calm, Cool & Collected")
Don't panic, don't rush, don't shout. Short sentences. Definite statements. Periods, not exclamation marks.

- ✅ "AI projects fail for predictable reasons. We've seen them. We help you avoid them."
- ❌ "Don't let your AI initiative become a statistic! Ensure success with Alphabyte!!"

## Voice attributes

From the guide: **confident, composed, direct, optimistic, forward-thinking, active, human & helpful.**

Translated into writing rules:

| Attribute | What it means | Watch out for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Confident | State things directly, don't hedge | "we think maybe," "we try to" |
| Composed | Calm sentence rhythm, no urgency theater | "!!!", "DON'T MISS," countdown framing |
| Direct | Get to the point in the first sentence | long throat-clearing intros |
| Optimistic | Frame work as moving forward, solvable | fear-mongering ("Fall behind!") |
| Forward-thinking | Look at what's next, not what's hot | chasing buzzwords (AGI, "agentic," etc.) |
| Active | Active voice, strong verbs | "solutions are provided," "it is thought that" |
| Human & Helpful | Sound like a person talking | corporate euphemisms, HR-speak |

## Writing principles (do these)

1. **Short sentences beat long ones.** If a sentence has three commas, it probably wants to be two sentences.
2. **Active voice.** "We ship this in six weeks" not "This is shipped by us in six weeks."
3. **Specific over generic.** "A retail client reduced returns by 18%" beats "Our clients see significant ROI."
4. **Contractions are fine.** "We're" reads more human than "we are."
5. **State, don't sell.** Describe what you do; let the reader decide if it's impressive.
6. **Use plain words.** "Use" not "utilize." "Help" not "empower." "Start" not "initiate." "About" not "regarding."
7. **Numbers when they exist.** "12 engineers" is more trustworthy than "a team of experts."
8. **Acknowledge difficulty.** "AI projects are hard to get right" builds trust. Pretending everything is easy doesn't.

## Anti-patterns (stop if you catch yourself)

### Forbidden vocabulary
Words that automatically sound like generic AI marketing. If you've typed one, rewrite.

- unlock / unleash / harness
- leverage / leveraging
- transform / transformative / transformation
- revolutionize / revolutionary / game-changing
- cutting-edge / state-of-the-art / next-generation / bleeding-edge
- disrupt / disruption / disruptive
- synergy / synergize / synergistic
- empower (except in the specific §1.0 brand-value sense, and even then, sparingly)
- world-class / best-in-class / industry-leading
- AI-powered / AI-driven / AI-first
- seamless / seamlessly
- mission-critical
- turnkey / end-to-end solution
- robust
- scalable (unless describing a specific technical property)

### Forbidden phrase patterns

- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "More than ever before..."
- "As [industry] continues to evolve..."
- "Don't get left behind."
- "The future of [thing] is here."
- "It's no secret that..."
- "At [company], we believe..."
- Any sentence starting with "In a world where..."

### Structural anti-patterns

- Headlines longer than 8 words
- Three-word alliterative taglines ("Innovate. Iterate. Inspire.")
- Rhetorical questions as headlines ("Ready to transform your business?")
- Stacked exclamation marks or any exclamation marks in marketing copy
- Em-dashes as drama ("We do one thing — and we do it *well*.")
- Bold text for emphasis in body copy (use italic per §4.0)

## Good vs. bad copy — worked examples

### Hero headline

❌ **Bad:** "Unlock the Transformative Power of AI for Your Enterprise"
✅ **Good:** "AI systems that actually ship."

❌ **Bad:** "Revolutionize Your Business with Cutting-Edge Machine Learning"
✅ **Good:** "We help companies put AI to work."

❌ **Bad:** "The Future of Data-Driven Decision Making Starts Here"
✅ **Good:** "Start with the data you already have."

### Service description

❌ **Bad:** "Our world-class team of AI experts partners with you to deliver transformative, end-to-end solutions that drive unprecedented ROI through cutting-edge machine learning."
✅ **Good:** "We pair senior engineers with your team for three to six months. You end with a production system, documentation, and your people trained to run it."

### CTA

❌ **Bad:** "Start Your AI Journey Today!"
✅ **Good:** "Book a call"

❌ **Bad:** "Unlock Your Potential — Get Started Now"
✅ **Good:** "Get in touch"

### About-us paragraph

❌ **Bad:** "Founded on the belief that AI can transform the way enterprises operate, Alphabyte is a passionate team of industry-leading experts dedicated to unlocking the full potential of artificial intelligence for our valued clients."

✅ **Good:** "We're an AI and data consultancy. We work with companies that have real data problems and want to solve them properly — not prototype something that never ships. We're small, senior, and deliberate about the work we take on."

## Reference copy from current direction (Option C)

These are exemplar headlines and sub-heads from the chosen design direction. Use them as calibration for tone, rhythm, and structure when writing new copy.

### Hero headlines

- **"AI that compounds. Not pilots that stall."** — homepage hero. Short, declarative, contrast structure.
- **"Most organizations don't have an AI problem. They have a clarity problem."** — services hero. Diagnostic framing, contrast structure.
- **"We build with what actually works."** — tools hero. Direct, slightly anti-establishment.

### Sub-heads

- **"We work across four tracks. You don't need all of them. You need the right one, in the right order, for where you actually are."** — services. Empathetic, qualifying, anti-bloat.
- **"Four tracks. Real deployments. No junior bench."** — homepage. Three short clauses, period-separated, asserts substance.

### Copy patterns to follow

When writing new headlines and sub-heads, these patterns recur:

1. **Two-sentence contrast:** the second sentence undercuts the first. "X. Not Y." — establishes what Alphabyte does by naming what it doesn't.
2. **Period-separated short clauses:** three brief statements separated by periods for credibility. "Fact. Fact. Fact."
3. **Diagnostic framing:** "You don't have an X problem. You have a Y problem." — reframes the reader's assumption.
4. **Direct anti-jargon stances:** "We don't sell platforms." "We build with what actually works." — positions against industry noise without naming competitors.

## Brand narrative (from the guide)

The official narrative, for reference:

> "When the only constant is change, we hold steady as the calm amidst the chaos. Rolling up our sleeves when the going gets tough (and even when it doesn't), navigating uncharted territories and seeing things others miss. We apply our deep technological expertise, data science methodologies, specialized know-how, and custom technologies to devise complete services and products. We ignite meaningful change that makes our clients — and their clients — lives better."

**How to use this:** don't quote it verbatim in marketing copy. It's a north star for tone, not headline material. When writing, ask "does this sound like the same company that wrote that narrative?" If the answer is "no, this sounds louder/hype-ier/more corporate," rewrite.

## Formatting & mechanics

- **Capitalization:** Title Case for headers (per §4.0), Sentence case for sub-headers and everything else.
- **Oxford comma:** use it. "Engineering, product, and design."
- **Numbers:** spell out one through nine in body copy; use digits for 10 and up and for all statistics.
- **Percentages:** always digits ("18%" not "eighteen percent").
- **Em-dashes:** sparingly. Prefer a period or a colon.
- **Italic:** for inline emphasis (per §4.0 Highlight spec). Don't use bold for emphasis in body copy.
- **Links:** descriptive link text, not "click here." Links get the Aeonik Regular styling in Alphabyte Blue per §4.0.

## When unsure

Write the sentence two ways — the hypey way and the plain way — and pick the plain one. If the plain version sounds too boring, you probably need more *specificity*, not more *volume*.
