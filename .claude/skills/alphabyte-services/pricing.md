# Pricing

This is the most consequential file in the skill. The reference document contains a full rate card by role, after-hours multipliers, effort estimates in hours, and detailed engagement pricing — almost none of which is for public consumption.

This file documents what is publishable and what is not, with explicit examples.

## Hard rule

**The rate card by role is NEVER published.** No exceptions. This applies to:

- All hourly rates (AI Developer $350, Senior Consultant $225, etc.)
- After-hours multipliers (1.5×)
- Emergency multipliers (2×)
- Blended rates ($200 consulting dev blended)
- Public sector rate variations
- Project Management rates at any level

If a feature wants to communicate "what does this cost?" — use **package ranges** or **starting at** framing. Never quote per-hour rates.

### Engagement model

These framings are publishable:
- "Fixed-price for productized offerings (Strategy Sprint, Executive Quick Win)"
- "Time and materials for custom builds"
- "Travel and expenses billed at cost"
- "Phased delivery with go/no-go checkpoints between phases" (for Intelligent Enterprise)

### Senior team statement

The fact that Alphabyte staffs engagements with senior people — "no junior bench" — is publishable as a positioning statement. **Do not** name competitors when stating it. Frame it as a positive statement about Alphabyte, not a comparison.

- ✅ "Engagements are staffed with senior consultants and developers. No junior bench."
- ❌ "Unlike Deloitte, we don't staff with junior consultants."

## What cannot be published

### Per-role hourly rates — confidential

The rate card lives in the reference doc. It contains:

- AI Developer: **regular rate**, after-hours, emergency rates
- Senior Consultant (Ontario, Public Sector): **regular rates**, after-hours, emergency
- Intermediate Consultant (Ontario, Public Sector): **regular rates**, after-hours, emergency
- Associate Consultant (Ontario, Public Sector): **regular rates**, after-hours, emergency
- Consulting Dev Blended Rate: **regular rate**, after-hours, emergency
- Program Management: **regular rate**, after-hours, emergency
- Project Management (Ontario, Public Sector): **regular rates**, after-hours, emergency
- Associate PM / Project Coordinator: **regular rate**, after-hours, emergency
- End User Training / Adoption Planning: **regular rate**

None of these go on the website. The website doesn't need to quote per-hour rates because pricing is communicated through engagement-scoped ranges.

### After-hours and emergency multipliers — confidential

These are SOW negotiation terms, not marketing content.

### Effort estimates in hours — confidential

The reference doc lists every offering with an effort range in hours (e.g., 64–90 hrs, 100–200 hrs). These are scoping numbers used in proposals. Translate to weeks when communicating publicly.

- ✅ "Discovery: 3–5 weeks"
- ❌ "Discovery: 64–90 hours of effort"

### Government incentive references — confidential

IRAP, CDAP, NSERC eligibility for public sector engagements — these are sales conversation points, not website content. They can flex pricing in the right contexts, but referencing them publicly puts the wrong frame around Alphabyte's offering.

### Specific deliverable counts — handle with care

The reference doc specifies things like "up to 10 stakeholder workshops" or "use case dev ×3" — these are SOW scoping commitments. Use them in marketing only as illustrative examples, not as fixed commitments.

- ✅ "Stakeholder workshops, use case prioritization, and a roadmap"
- ⚠️ "Up to 10 stakeholder workshops" — okay if accompanied by context that this is illustrative; not okay if it reads as a guaranteed deliverable
- ❌ Listing every numbered deliverable from the reference doc verbatim

## Pricing language patterns

When writing pricing-related copy, prefer these patterns:

### Pattern 1: Range with anchor
"Discovery engagements run 3–5 weeks at $14K–$22.5K, fixed price."

### Pattern 2: Starting at
"Executive Productivity Suite: starting at $7K. Two to four weeks to deploy."

### Pattern 3: Investment framing for larger work
"Custom AI Agents engagements typically run 8–16 weeks. Investment varies by complexity; most fall in the $35K–$90K range."

### Pattern 4: Decline to quote
For top-of-funnel pages, sometimes the right answer is *not* showing pricing at all.
"Pricing depends on scope and tier. Book a Strategy Sprint to scope yours."

This is a legitimate move — Alphabyte's mid-market clients are typically used to scoping conversations rather than menu pricing. Don't force prices into copy that doesn't need them.

## Pricing on the Services pages — guidance

The Services overview page benefits from showing approximate ranges so visitors can self-qualify ("am I in this neighborhood?"). Per-track detail pages should show ranges for the specific offerings on that page.

The Tools pages probably **don't** need pricing — they describe technology stack, not engagement scope. Tools are sold inside Services engagements, not à la carte.

The homepage probably **doesn't** need pricing — it's positioning, not a quote.

The CTAs that lead to pricing conversations are: "Book a Strategy Sprint" (for Discovery-level engagement) and "Book a call" (for everything else).

## Red flags

- About to write `$350/hour` or `$225/hour` or any specific per-hour rate
- About to publish the after-hours or emergency multipliers
- About to quote effort in hours rather than weeks
- About to list every numbered deliverable from the reference doc
- About to mention IRAP, CDAP, or NSERC on the website
- About to compare Alphabyte's pricing to a named competitor

If any of these appear in draft copy, stop and revise before shipping.
